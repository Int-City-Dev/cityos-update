02 / 07

The research

For thirty years, software had a memory. The physical world didn’t.

Independent work is converging on a user-owned context layer.

The academic case

The Human Context Protocol (Stanford Digital Economy Lab with Consumer Reports, Dec 2025) specifies a portable, user-governed preference layer with scoped, revocable, minimal disclosure.

Authors from MIT, Stanford, Oxford (UK AI Security Institute), Microsoft Research, and OpenMined.

Europe is already shipping it

GDPR Article 20 makes data portability a legal right, and Flanders already provisions user-owned data vaults for ~6.5M citizens on the open Solid standard (Inrupt, co-founded by Tim Berners-Lee).

The regulation and the first civic-scale deployment are both European. The thesis is past theory.

And the economics say incumbents won’t build it: lock-in, data hoarding, and market power all reward retaining context, not releasing it. The openings were forced before, by number portability and open banking.

But every one of them stops at the digital edge. None captures the real world.

Two halves of one layer. Only one is being built.

The context layer sits between the AI models above and the devices below, and it splits cleanly in two.

Digital · HCP, Inrupt

What I want, across my AIs.

  • Stated preferences and credentials
  • Ported between models and services
  • Being built, by the research and Europe

Physical · CityOS

Who I am and what’s around me, across places.

  • Real-world context that builds as you move
  • Places and hardware that react, with consent
  • Open ground. This is what we build.

Same open standards (HCP, MCP). One assistant reads both, only what you allow, and the share closes when you leave.

When places can recognize you and react, with consent, an entire new category of products becomes possible, and most will be built by others. CityOS is the layer they build on: apps, merchant tools, civic infrastructure, and personal relays, all calling the same network.
